Our Story

A legacy of quality and craftsmanship since 1870

Rooted in Vermont's Maple Tradition

For over 150 years, Bolton's Vermont Distilling Company has been crafting exceptional beers that celebrate Vermont's most famous natural resource: pure maple syrup. What began as a small family operation has grown into a cooperative of passionate brewers and distillers, all dedicated to preserving our heritage while innovating for the future.

Our Journey Through Time

1870

Maple Whiskey Beginnings

Chester Bolton founds Bolton's Vermont Distilling Company, crafting premium maple syrup whiskey using time-honored techniques and local Vermont ingredients.

1930

New Ownership

Following Prohibition, the Bolton family's involvement in the company ends as new owners take over, marking the beginning of a new chapter for the distillery.

1935

Brewing Begins

The company shifts focus from whiskey to beer production, developing unique maple-infused recipes that would become their signature brews.

1955

Worker Cooperative

Employees form a worker cooperative to purchase the company, ensuring local ownership and preserving the Bolton's legacy for future generations.

2000s

Modern Renaissance

Bolton's gains recognition for its innovative maple-infused beers, winning awards and expanding distribution throughout New England while maintaining small-batch quality.

2015

Sustainability Leadership

The cooperative implements comprehensive sustainability initiatives, including solar power and zero-waste production, setting new standards for environmental responsibility in craft brewing.

Present

Looking Forward

Today, Bolton's remains worker-owned and community-focused, continuing to innovate while honoring our heritage of quality and craftsmanship in every bottle.
