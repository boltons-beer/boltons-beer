Our Owners

Meet the people behind Bolton's Vermont Distilling Company

Proudly Employee-Owned

In 1985, Bolton's transitioned to a worker-owned cooperative, ensuring that the people who make our products share in the company's success. Today, our cooperative is made up of passionate individuals who are committed to crafting exceptional maple-infused beers while upholding our values of quality, sustainability, and community.

Our Cooperative Principles

As a worker-owned cooperative, we adhere to the seven cooperative principles that guide our business and our relationships with our members and community.

1

Voluntary & Open Membership

Our cooperative is open to all who want to join and are willing to accept the responsibilities of membership.

2

Democratic Member Control

Each member-owner has an equal say in how we run our businessâ€”one member, one vote.

3

Member Economic Participation

Members contribute equitably to, and democratically control, the capital of the cooperative.

4

Autonomy & Independence

We are an independent organization controlled by our members, not outside investors.

5

Education & Training

We provide education and training for our members, elected representatives, and employees.

6

Cooperation Among Cooperatives

We work together with other cooperatives to strengthen the cooperative movement.

7

Concern for Community

We work for the sustainable development of our communities through policies accepted by our members.
